💡 Tips, Mnemonics & Motivation

Study smarter. Remember more. Pass with confidence.

📅
Create a Study Schedule
Divide your review into 12–16 weeks. Allocate 2–3 days per subject, with weekends for full mock exams and weak-area review. Consistency beats cramming.
🎯
Focus on High-Yield Topics
Med-Surg accounts for the largest portion of NLE questions. Prioritize cardiovascular, respiratory, and neurological nursing. Mastering these three alone can significantly boost your score.
📖
Active Recall Over Passive Reading
Don't just re-read notes. Test yourself constantly. Use the flashcards and quizzes here. Active recall doubles retention compared to highlighting.
📊
Track Your Weak Areas
After each quiz, note which subjects you scored below 70%. Dedicate extra study time there. The NLE requires no subject below 60% — don't neglect any area.
💤
Prioritize Sleep During Review
Sleep consolidates memory. Aim for 7–8 hours nightly. The week before the exam, reduce new material and focus on light review. Never pull an all-nighter before exam day.
⏱️
Practice Timed Questions
Each PNLE subject gives you ~85 minutes for 85 items — roughly 1 minute per question. Use this site's mock exam to practice pacing. If stuck, make your best educated guess and move on.
🔤
Read Questions Carefully
Look for key words: "first," "priority," "immediate," "most important," "best." These change the correct answer. Always answer what is asked — not what you wish was asked.
📝
Master the Nursing Process
When in doubt, apply ADPIE. Assessment always comes before intervention. Planning before implementation. If a question asks what to do first, look for an assessment answer unless a life-threatening situation demands immediate action.
🗓️
Suggested 12-Week Study Plan
Weeks 1–2: Fundamentals of Nursing. Weeks 3–4: Medical-Surgical I (Cardiovascular, Respiratory). Weeks 5–6: Medical-Surgical II (Neuro, Endo, Renal). Week 7: Maternal & Child. Week 8: Psychiatric Nursing. Week 9: Community Health. Week 10: Leadership & Jurisprudence. Weeks 11–12: Full mock exams, weak-area review, and final polish.
🩺
Elimination Strategy
When unsure, eliminate options that are clearly wrong. Then choose the most "nursing" answer — the one that prioritizes safety, addresses the patient's priority need, and follows the nursing process. Avoid answers that involve just calling the doctor unless assessment is already done.
ADPIE Nursing Process
Assessment → Diagnosis → Planning → Implementation → Evaluation
MONA MI Treatment
Morphine → Oxygen → Nitrates → Aspirin — given in this order for myocardial infarction
FAST Stroke Recognition
Facial drooping → Arm weakness → Speech difficulty → Time to call (emergency)
APGAR Newborn Assessment
Appearance (color) → Pulse → Grimace (reflex) → Activity (muscle tone) → Respiration. Scored 0–2 each, total 0–10.
ROME ABG Interpretation
Respiratory Opposite — in respiratory disorders, pH and CO₂ move in OPPOSITE directions. Metabolic Equal — in metabolic disorders, pH and HCO₃ move in the SAME direction.
TICOSM Chain of Infection
The Infectious Cause Often Spreads Marvelously — Infectious Agent → Reservoir → Portal of Exit → Mode of Transmission → Portal of Entry → Susceptible Host
3 P's of Diabetes DM Signs
Polydipsia (excessive thirst) · Polyphagia (excessive hunger) · Polyuria (excessive urination)
DIGFAST Mania (Bipolar)
Distractibility · Irresponsibility/Impulsivity · Grandiosity · Flight of ideas · Activity increased · Sleep decreased · Talkativeness
SIG E CAPS Depression Symptoms
Sleep changes · Interest lost · Guilt · Energy decreased · Concentration poor · Appetite changes · Psychomotor changes · Suicidal ideation
I WATCH DEATH Delirium Causes
Infection · Withdrawal · Acute metabolic · Trauma · CNS pathology · Hypoxia · Deficiencies · Endocrinopathy · Acute vascular · Toxins/drugs · Heavy metals
LATCH Score Breastfeeding
Latch · Audible swallowing · Type of nipple · Comfort · Hold/positioning. Scored 0–2 each; score of 8–10 = good breastfeeding.
SBAR Communication
Situation · Background · Assessment · Recommendation — the standard framework for nurse-to-physician communication
9 Rights of Medication Pharmacology
Right Patient · Right Drug · Right Dose · Right Route · Right Time · Right Documentation · Right Reason · Right Response · Right to Refuse
RACE Fire Safety
Rescue patients → Activate alarm → Confine fire (close doors) → Extinguish / Evacuate
PASS Fire Extinguisher
Pull the pin → Aim at base of fire → Squeeze handle → Sweep side to side

📦 What to Bring on Exam Day

✅ Required

  • Notice of Admission (printed)
  • One valid government-issued ID with photo
  • 2–4 pencils (No. 2 or HB)
  • Eraser (good quality)
  • Ball pen (for signature)

💡 Recommended

  • Water and light snacks
  • Watch (non-smart)
  • Light jacket/sweater (AC)
  • Arrive 30 min early
  • Leave phone at home or car
📐 Passing Criteria
  • Overall weighted average of 75% across all 6 subjects
  • No individual subject score below 60%
  • Total items: 510 (85 per subject)
  • Rating = (Sum of all subject scores) ÷ 6
Passing = Overall avg ≥ 75% AND each subject ≥ 60%
